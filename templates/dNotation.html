👀 Reading Sheet Music

Reading sheet music is like reading a map for music. Here's what to look for:

  1. Clef – Most common is the treble clef (𝄞), which tells you how to read the notes on the staff.
  2. Notes & Rests – Tell you what to play and when to be silent.
  3. Bar Lines – Vertical lines that break the music into small groups of beats, called measures.
  4. Key Signature – Shows which notes are sharp (#) or flat (♭) throughout the piece.

Think of sheet music as instructions for what to play, when to play, and how long to play each sound or silence.
